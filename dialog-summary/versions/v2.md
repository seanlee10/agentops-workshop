# Dialogue Summary Skill

## Description

Use this skill when the user asks you to summarize a short dialogue, chat transcript, messenger conversation, or meeting-like exchange.

## Goal

Create a concise summary that captures only the main point(s) of the conversation, including important decisions, requests, plans, or emotional context when directly relevant.

## Input

The input is usually a speaker-labeled dialogue. Each line may contain a speaker name followed by their message.

Example:

Alice: Are you coming to dinner tonight?  
Bob: I might be late because of work.  
Alice: That's okay. I'll save you some food.

## Procedure

1. Read the entire dialogue carefully before summarizing.
2. Identify all main participants and ensure their names are spelled exactly as in the dialogue.
3. Determine the main topic and purpose of the conversation.
4. Extract only the most important decisions, requests, plans, problems, or commitments.
5. Exclude greetings, small talk, filler expressions, repeated information, and unnecessary emotional details unless they directly affect the main point.
6. Do **not** add, infer, or interpret information, motivations, or feelings that are not explicitly stated in the dialogue.
7. Avoid including extra context, explanations, or details that are not central to the main point.
8. Focus on brevity: include only the essential information needed to convey the main outcome or decision.
9. Write a concise summary in natural language, aiming for maximum clarity and minimal length.

## Output Format

Write only the final summary.

The summary should be 1–3 sentences.

## Rules

* Use the exact names, dates, times, and places as stated in the dialogue; do not alter or misspell them.
* Do not add, infer, or interpret information, motivations, or feelings that are not directly supported by the dialogue.
* Attribute actions, statements, and intentions to the correct speakers.
* Include concrete plans, requests, or decisions if present.
* Exclude details that do not change the main outcome or decision, even if they provide extra context.
* Prefer a clear third-person summary.
* Do not quote the dialogue unless the exact wording is essential for meaning.
* Keep the summary as brief as possible while preserving all essential information.

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing, check:

* Does the summary capture only the main point(s) of the conversation, without extra context or detail?
* Are all names, dates, times, and places accurate and spelled correctly?
* Are the speakers' intentions and actions represented precisely, without added or inferred interpretation?
* Are concrete plans, requests, or decisions included if present?
* Is irrelevant small talk or unnecessary detail excluded?
* Is the summary concise and within 1–3 sentences?
